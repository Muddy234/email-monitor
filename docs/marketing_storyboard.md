# Clarion AI — Marketing Storyboard

**Brand:** Clarion AI
**Tagline:** Your inbox, handled. Before your first meeting.
**Target audience:** Real estate, finance, consulting, and legal professionals
**Tone:** Confident, warm, professional. No jargon. Emphasize time reclaimed, not technology.

---

## Act 1: The Problem (Tension)

### Scene 1 — "The Morning Scroll"
**Visual:** Close-up of a phone on a nightstand, alarm going off at 6:45 AM. A hand reaches for it. Cut to a thumb scrolling through an Outlook inbox — the unread count reads **127**.

**Text overlay:** *"127 new emails. Before coffee."*

**Voiceover / caption:** "Every morning starts the same way. Scroll. Flag. Scroll. Flag. Which ones actually need you?"

---

### Scene 2 — "The Wrong Priority"
**Visual:** Split-screen. Left side: the user composing a long reply to a vendor newsletter. Right side: a time-sensitive email from a client buried 40 messages down, unread and unanswered.

**Text overlay:** *"You replied to the wrong email first. Again."*

**Voiceover / caption:** "Without a system, urgency is a guessing game. The emails that matter most are the ones you find last."

### Scene 3 — "The Meeting Starts"
**Visual:** Laptop open to a half-written email. A calendar notification slides in: *"Weekly standup — starting now."* The user closes the laptop. The draft disappears.

**Text overlay:** *"45 minutes gone. Zero replies sent."*

**Voiceover / caption:** "2.6 hours a day. That's 28% of your work week — spent managing email, not doing work."

## Act 2: The Setup (Resolution Begins)

### Scene 4 — "60 Seconds"
**Visual:** Clean, bright screen recording. Chrome Web Store page. A single click: "Add to Chrome." The Clarion AI icon appears in the toolbar. The user clicks it, types an email and password, clicks "Sign Up." Done.

**Text overlay:** *"60 seconds. No IT ticket. No admin approval. No API keys."*

**Voiceover / caption:** "Clarion AI works at the browser layer — the same way you already read email. If you can open Outlook, you can use Clarion."

**Key message:** Zero-friction install. No IT gatekeepers.

---

### Scene 5 — "It's Already Learning"
**Visual:** Animated progress bar or subtle pulsing visualization. Status labels appear in sequence: "Analyzing your inbox..." → "Mapping your contacts..." → "Learning your writing style..."

Behind the animation, a counter ticks up: *"1,247 emails analyzed. 86 contacts profiled. 1 writing style captured."*

**Text overlay:** *"Clarion reads 4 months of your email history. Not to store it — to understand you."*

**Voiceover / caption:** "In the background, Clarion is doing something no other email tool does. It's reading your sent emails to learn how *you* write. Your tone. Your sign-offs. Your level of formality with each person. It's mapping your relationships — who matters most, who you always reply to, who can wait. By the time you finish your first cup of coffee, Clarion knows your inbox better than you do."

**Key messages:**
- The onboarding is invisible — the user doesn't configure anything
- 120 days of history = deep understanding from day one
- Sent email analysis = drafts that sound like you, not like a robot
- Contact intelligence = it knows your client from your newsletter

## Key Differentiators to Emphasize Across All Materials

| Message | Why it matters | Proof point |
|---|---|---|
| **"Sounds like you, not AI"** | Biggest objection to AI email tools | Onboarding analyzes 120 days of sent emails to build a personal writing style guide |
| **"Knows your relationships"** | Generic tools treat every sender the same | Contact intelligence maps 86+ contacts with relationship significance, reply patterns, response times |
| **"No IT approval needed"** | Enterprise buyers stall on security review | Browser-layer approach — no OAuth, no API keys, no admin permissions |
| **"Works while you sleep"** | Competitors require manual triggering | Background sync every 5 min + real-time draft delivery to Outlook Drafts folder |
| **"Gets smarter over time"** | Users fear a static tool | Scoring model retrains automatically as behavior patterns change |
| **"Drafts appear in Outlook"** | No new app to learn, no new workflow | Drafts land directly in the user's existing Drafts folder via OWA |

## Brand Guidelines for Materials

- **Product name:** Clarion AI (always capitalized, always with "AI")
- **Company:** Clarion AI
- **Never say:** "reads your email for you" (implies surveillance) — instead say "analyzes" or "understands"
- **Never say:** "AI writes your emails" — instead say "drafts replies in your voice for your review"
- **Always emphasize:** The human stays in control. Review, edit, send — or discard
- **Privacy framing:** "Works at the browser layer" — not "accesses your email"
- **Target verticals:** Real estate, finance, consulting, legal (high email volume, relationship-driven)
